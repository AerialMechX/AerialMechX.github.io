About & Vision

I'm a Master's student in Robotics at IIIT Hyderabad, working at the intersection of control theory and intelligent autonomy. My research focuses on making aerial robots capable of physically interacting with the world — not just flying through it.

Before joining IIIT, I spent a year at Arka Aerospace designing autonomous UAV navigation systems, multi-drone coordination algorithms, and precision landing pipelines with 2–3 cm accuracy.

I believe the next frontier of robotics is Physical AI — systems that combine rigorous control theory with learned perception to operate reliably in unstructured, dynamic environments without human oversight.

Long-Term Goal

Develop aerial robotic systems capable of autonomous physical intervention in hazardous environments — disaster response, infrastructure inspection, and remote manipulation — eliminating the need for human presence in dangerous situations.

Hierarchical RL + Adaptive INDI for Aerial Manipulation

Reinforcement Learning INDI Control ROS 2 PPO
Active — Jan 2026
Watch Demo Add YouTube link to activate

A hierarchical flight-control stack for an underactuated aerial manipulator. An outer-loop RL policy outputs thrust and body-rate commands, while the inner-loop Adaptive INDI (running at 250 Hz) handles rapid attitude stabilization and disturbance rejection during manipulation transients.

View on GitHub
Key Achievements
  • Domain-randomized PPO policy (payload mass, inertia, actuator variation)
  • Online control-effectiveness adaptation via filtered angular-acceleration feedback
  • Real-world grasp-and-drop validation with max 20 cm end-effector error
  • Robust sim-to-real transfer across manipulation transients
Current Status

Active development — Conducting real-world flight tests. Currently refining reward shaping for smoother grasp transitions and reducing tracking error below 15 cm.

Future Goals
  • Extend to multi-arm configurations on a single UAV
  • Incorporate force/torque sensing for contact-rich manipulation
  • Target submission to IROS / RA-L 2026

Autonomous Language-Guided Aerial Grasping

Vision-Language CNN-ViT SAM-3 6-DoF Grasping
Active — Jan 2026
Watch Demo Add YouTube link to activate

A modular framework enabling aerial manipulators to carry out language-guided grasping in cluttered, unstructured environments. The system processes natural language commands to identify, localize, and grasp target objects — autonomously handling occlusions via orbital search maneuvers.

Key Achievements
  • Lightweight hybrid CNN-ViT predicts 6-DoF grasp poses in under 38 ms
  • Hierarchical Risk-Aware Decision Framework filters kinematic reachability
  • Real-time SAM-3 tracking with autonomous occlusion-clearing search
  • End-to-end pipeline from language input to physical grasp execution
Current Status

Active development — Integrating language model front-end with grasp prediction pipeline. Conducting cluttered-environment validation tests.

Future Goals
  • Generalize to open-vocabulary object categories
  • Deploy on resource-constrained onboard compute (Jetson Orin)
  • Enable multi-step, dialog-driven manipulation tasks

Adaptive Control for Multi-Quadrotor Formations

Distributed Control Adaptive Laws Multi-UAV UUB Stability
Active — Jul 2025
Watch Demo Add YouTube link to activate

A distributed adaptive control strategy for multi-quadrotor formations encountering strong, unknown interconnection forces. Nonlinear adaptive laws handle state-dependent uncertainties including rotor downwash and aerodynamic drag — without requiring prior model knowledge or boundedness assumptions.

Key Achievements
  • Proven Uniformly Ultimately Bounded (UUB) stability for entire closed-loop system
  • Real-world cooperative aerial transportation experiments
  • No prior boundedness or model structure required
  • Handles rotor downwash and complex aerodynamic coupling
Current Status

Active — Scaling to 3+ UAV formations. Preparing real-world payload transportation experiments with varying load distributions.

Future Goals
  • Scale to heterogeneous swarms (fixed-wing + multirotor)
  • Add communication-delay robustness
  • Apply to large-structure aerial assembly tasks

RRT + MPC Quadrotor Autonomy Stack

Model Predictive Control RRT Planning 100 Hz Control Aggressive Flight
Feb – May 2025
Watch Demo Add YouTube link to activate

A layered autonomy stack where RRT generates collision-free geometric routes, a trajectory module converts them into time-parameterized references suitable for aggressive flight, and a real-time MPC controller optimizes collective thrust and body-rate commands within actuator limits — achieving stable tracking at 100 Hz.

Key Achievements
  • 100 Hz closed-loop MPC optimization on hardware
  • Stable tracking of aggressive maneuvers (tight turns, fast accelerations)
  • Efficient warm-starting and simplified dynamics for real-time performance
  • Full end-to-end collision-free planning to execution pipeline
Current Status

Completed — Core system validated. Results informing the hierarchical RL + INDI project's inner-loop architecture.

Future Goals
  • Integrate learning-based dynamics models into MPC prediction
  • Extend RRT to dynamic obstacle environments
  • Apply to indoor inspection scenarios with GPS denial

Autonomous Ball Catcher — "The Robotic Dustbin"

Stereo Vision RANSAC PID Drive UDP Streaming
Oct – Dec 2025
Watch Demo Add YouTube link to activate

An autonomous mobile robot that intercepts thrown balls in real time using an external multi-camera 3D world frame — no onboard vision required. HSV-based detection, stereo triangulation, and physics-based RANSAC trajectory estimation predict landing coordinates. The robot drives to the impact point via 30 Hz UDP-streamed commands before the ball lands.

View on GitHub
Key Achievements
  • Real-time stereo triangulation with HSV ball detection
  • Physics-based RANSAC estimation robust to noisy measurements
  • Low-latency 30 Hz UDP control loop (Raspberry Pi + Arduino)
  • PID-controlled differential-drive motion for accurate interception
Current Status

Completed — System validated in real-world tests. Achieves reliable interception across a range of throw speeds and trajectories.

Future Goals
  • Move to onboard vision for full autonomy
  • Extend to multiple simultaneous projectile tracking
  • Apply estimation framework to aerial catching with UAVs

Publications

2025 — ICCAS · International Conference on Control, Automation and Systems

Impedance and Stability Targeted Adaptation for Aerial Manipulator with Unknown Coupling Dynamics

Developed an adaptive impedance control system for unknown coupling forces and non-linear dynamics during contact-based aerial tasks. Introduced a novel adaptation law ensuring compliant behavior in unstructured environments without accurate vehicle-arm interaction models. Demonstrates high-performance stability in aerial perching and object-catching scenarios where conventional controllers fail.

Amitabh Sharma · Saksham Gupta · Shivansh Pratap Singh · Rishabh Dev Yadav · Hongyu Song · Wei Pan · Spandan Roy · Simone Baldi

2025 — Preprint / Under Review

AERMANI-Diffusion: Regime-Conditioned Diffusion for Dynamics Learning in Aerial Manipulators

Created AERMANI-Diffusion — a regime-conditioned diffusion model capturing non-stationary dynamics of aerial manipulators under environmental changes. A time-of-day encoded module rapidly predicts residual forces under varying loading conditions. Validated through real-world flight tests demonstrating enhanced tracking accuracy and stability during high-acceleration dynamic scenarios.

Shared First Author: Samaksh Ujjawal* · Shivansh Pratap Singh* · Naveen Sudheer · Rishabh Dev Yadav · Wei Pan · Spandan Roy
